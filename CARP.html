Contrast, Alignment, Repetition and Proximity

C.A.R.P is a set of design principles

The task at hand was to design posters for a film festival called True/ False, whilst thinking about these design principles.

C.A.R.P summarised

  • Contrast - When two clashing/ opposite elements (ex. dark & light, bold & thin) are used together in a design to synergise and compliment eachother.

  • Alignment - The way contents are layed out in a design to visually connect elements.

  • Repetition - Consistency in design. An ongoing pattern of presenting the contents to visually differ the different contents and aid understanding.

  • Proximity - The way contents are placed together to show they are related and clarify the different sections of the design.
