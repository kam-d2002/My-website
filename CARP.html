Design Fundamentals - Kameron Dancey

Contrast, Alignment, Repetition and Proximity

C.A.R.P is a set of design principles

The task at hand was to design posters for a film festival called True/ False, whilst thinking about these design principles.

C.A.R.P summarised

  • Contrast - When two clashing/ opposite elements (ex. dark & light, bold & thin) are used together in a design to compliment eachother.

  • Alignment - The way contents are layed out in a design, what angle they are at etc, can be used to visually connect elements.

  • Repetition - Consistency in design. An ongoing pattern of presenting the contents to visually differ the different contents and aid understanding.

  • Proximity - The way contents are placed together to show they are related and clarify the different sections of the design.

    • Initial designs.

    These are the first 3 pieces I designed but there was much more improvement to be done.

    When thinking about contrast, I thought it would be good to include different font styles (blocky, curved etc). Also, I made sure to include some white to balance the dark colours. I successfully implemented the proximity principal in the first two posters by connecting related information together with the layout. In reflection, the placement of text could be improved and there could be more proximity in the upper section of the poster. Alignment was implemented all through the design but with feedback and reflection, I concluded i'd aligned the contents too much and need to work on this. Moving on, I needed to make sure to implement the repetition principal and work on improving use of the other principals. It was also suggested to add images to the film, so this was experimented with too.

    Poster development.

    I played with the layout to remove a bit of the excessive orderliness, I made the logo text come in from different sides and was happy with how this looked. I decided to make the slogan smaller to "marry" it in with the logo, this was to improve the proximity and it seemed fitting to have the slogan go below the logo. The varying fonts also provide contrast. I felt it was necessary to change the website name to the same font as the date and location to implement the repetition principal, this is to relate these two pieces to the audience as they are both key information on the page. I placed an image in the lower part of the poster and thought this didn't look great so removed this. I decided the film looked better split up to add a sectioned look to the page so it breaks the contents up a bit. I played with the placement of the website address to see where it would fit in nicely. I decided to put it on an angle to further mess with the previous orderly look. During this, also implementing ideas of alignment as it placed on the angled line of the film camera vector. I messed with the sizing of the top contents to see if that would improve the look and bring the content together nicely. I was happy with the result so I decided to keep it. The next step was to experiment with adding images to the film for the final poster.

    Final design.

    I finally settled on this design, I placed some relevant images into the film and made the layout more interesting and less orderly. I am now going to summarise my implementation of the C.A.R.P design principles.

    Contrast - The varied text styles/ shape and usage of bright/ dark colours provides contrast.
    Alignment - This principal was thoroughly experimented with when placing the text/ contents in. I discovered alignment can be a good or bad thing depending on the scenario.
    Repetition - This principal was implemented in the form of signifying certain information with the same font, to symbolise to the user which information is related/ important.
    Proximity - The grouping of information provides proximity, making the poster clearer to intepret for the user.


    Reflection on task.

    This task was brilliant for developing our design skills and learning to work to a professional standard. It is important to consider design principles whenever you are working on a piece so it has been handy to practice this. I am happy with my final result, I feel I experimented with the poster to a sufficient level and considered a good variation of layout ideas. The experimentation and feedback helped me reach a nicely developed outcome to add to my graphic design portfolio. Also, in conclusion I think I've definitely become more comfortable with the world of design principles and I am happy with my implementation of them.
