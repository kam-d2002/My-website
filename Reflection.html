Design Fundamentals - Kameron Dancey

Overall, I am very happy and proud of my results in this semester. Going from learning the basics of HTML and CSS to having my own fully functional website in the space of a few months has been amazing and I’ve gained valuable skills in the process. For example, flexbox rundown taught us the fundamentals of page grids and layout and served as a valuable tool for building our own websites. We also learnt how to embed external style sheets with adobe fonts, so we could add an exciting flair to our pages. It was nice to be able to present our work in a way that allows us to show our personal artistic design flairs. In combination with a rounded presentation of what we have learnt, I feel my website is a really good summary of my learning in this module so far. The workshops also gave us loads of new skills in relation to CSS. Primarily, teaching us how to make classes to alter specific page sections, change text sizes and colours etc. However, more excitingly I learnt how to use CSS to do some really fun alterations to my site contents. For example, the navigation buttons. We learnt how to use CSS to give the buttons motion and allow them to change colour when the mouse hovers over. Another important skill we learnt was embedding links. This was essential for navigation to the separate HTML pages and to take the user to our Github Respositories. Moving on, the C.A.R.P and typographic system tasks were a great insight into the graphic design world. Moving away from the technialities of code, we got to get in touch with our graphic designer side, learning how to use design principles and fundamental ways to layout text. These tasks definitely helped me explore my design ability and improve on it, gaining new skills. One of my favourite things to do with the page was colour schemes. I made sure to include a variety of colours throughout the different pages. This was to capture the feel of each theme. In the process though, I made sure not to over do it so there wasn’t any colour clashing, I am happy with the look of each page and I feel the colours compliment eachother nicely. To improve, I would of liked to have altered the UX page layout to a better standard and also properly implemented the carousel image viewer (althought it works, it could of worked better) but unfortunately time wasn’t on my side towards the end. Another thing I would of liked to added was a youtube video link, just to present this in the site. I did learn how to do this in the lesson so I can make sure to include a youtube link in a future website. Although there is definite room for improvement, I don’t feel I have reached a negative outcome and I am really happy with what I have achieved overall. This module has been very interesting and I look forward to the next semester!
